What is Optical Fiber?

An Optical fiber is a cylindrical fiber of glass, which is hair-thin in size, or any transparent dielectric medium.

Optical fiber is a technology that transmits information as light pulses along a glass or plastic fiber.

Note:-(µm=micron)

  • Core Diameter: 5 µm to 100 µm
  • Cladding Diameter: 125 µm
  • Jacket Diameter (Buffer Coating): 250 µm to 900 µm



Core: The inner part of the optical fiber, it is the ultra-thin center made of an optically transparent dielectric medium and carries the light transmitted to the receiver.

Cladding: The outer optical material surrounding the core, having a refractive index lower than the core. Cladding helps to keep the light within the core through the phenomenon of total internal reflection.

Coating or Plastic Jacket: It is a plastic coating that protects the fibers and is made of silicon rubber. The diameter of the coating is 250-300 µm.

Types of Optical Fibers:

Single Mode: This fiber is used for long-distance transmission of signals.

Multi Mode: These fibers are used for short-distance transmission of signals.
